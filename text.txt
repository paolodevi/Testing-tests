0. Framing: Why This Problem Is Subtle (Deep View)

The coupling problem is fundamentally:

> A projection between two incompatible discretizations that must preserve virtual work.



Mathematically:

Structural space: 

Aerodynamic space: 


You are constructing an operator:




With constraint:

Energy conservation:


\delta W = \mathbf{F}_a^T \mathbf{u}_a = \mathbf{F}_s^T \mathbf{u}_s

This is what separates:

engineering interpolation ❌
from

aeroelastic coupling ✅


👉 Emphasize this early—it reframes everything.

1. Full Aeroelastic Loop (Deeper Mechanics)

The coupled system in frequency domain:

\left[ -\omega^2 \mathbf{M} + i\omega \mathbf{C} + \mathbf{K} \right]\mathbf{q}
= \mathbf{Q}_a(\mathbf{q}, \omega)

Where:




Break this down in your lecture:

Chain of transformations:

1. Modal → physical:



\mathbf{u}_s = \mathbf{\Phi} \mathbf{q}

2. Structural → aerodynamic:



\mathbf{u}_a = \mathbf{H} \mathbf{u}_s

3. Aerodynamic loads:



\mathbf{F}_a = \mathbf{A}(\omega)\mathbf{u}_a

4. Back to structure:



\mathbf{F}_s = \mathbf{H}^T \mathbf{F}_a

5. Projection to modal space:



\mathbf{Q}_a = \mathbf{\Phi}^T \mathbf{F}_s

👉 Key insight for your team:

> The spline matrix  appears twice → errors are squared in effect.

 = Aerodynamic Influence Coefficient matrix

5. Splining – Deep Technical Breakdown


---

5.1 General Formulation

Most splines solve:

\min \left( ||H \mathbf{u}_s - \mathbf{u}_a||^2 + \lambda \, \text{smoothness penalty} \right)

OR enforce:

exact interpolation + minimum energy



---

5.2 Thin Plate Splines (TPS)

Energy functional:

E = \iint \left( 
\left(\frac{\partial^2 w}{\partial x^2}\right)^2 +
2\left(\frac{\partial^2 w}{\partial x \partial y}\right)^2 +
\left(\frac{\partial^2 w}{\partial y^2}\right)^2
\right) dxdy

👉 Interpretation:

Penalizes curvature

Produces globally smooth surface



---

Strength

Smooth aerodynamic deformation

Good for lifting surfaces


Weakness

Global support → every node affects entire surface

Bad for:

hinges

control surface gaps




---

5.3 Finite Plate Splines (FPS)

Discrete version of TPS:

Localized influence

Better conditioning



---

Practical parameters

Influence radius

Weighting functions


👉 This is where engineering judgment matters most.


---

5.4 RBE-based Methods

RBE2

Exact kinematic constraint

Over-constrains system


→ artificially stiff wing


---

RBE3

Weighted average

No stiffness added


→ better for load transfer


---

Deep issue

RBE methods:

Do not enforce smoothness

Can introduce:

discontinuities

unrealistic local deformation




---

5.5 Radial Basis Functions (RBF)

Modern approach:

H_{ij} = \phi(||x_i - x_j||)

Common kernels:

Gaussian

Multiquadric

Thin plate



---

Advantages

Meshless

Flexible

Good for complex geometries



---

Critical limitation

Conditioning of matrix

Choice of kernel parameter



---

5.6 Key Failure Modes (VERY IMPORTANT)

1. Extrapolation

Aero nodes outside structural convex hull → wild displacements



---

2. Over-smoothing

→ under-predicts torsion


---

3. Local noise amplification

→ corrupts aerodynamic loads


---

4. Rank deficiency

→ singular spline matrix

6. Patching – Deeper View


---

6.1 Why global splines fail

Real aircraft:

wings + fuselage + control surfaces


Different physics per region



---

6.2 Patching strategy

Divide domain:

Wing

Flap

Tail


Each gets:

its own spline



---

Interface condition problem

At patch boundaries:

displacement discontinuity

force imbalance



---

6.3 Advanced methods

Mortar methods

Weak enforcement of continuity

Variationally consistent


Used in:

high-fidelity CFD–FEM coupling



---

Trade-off

Method	Accuracy	Complexity

Global spline	medium	low
Patching	high	medium
Mortar	very high	high
